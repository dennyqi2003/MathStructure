Exercise 1.14 If $z$ is a complex number such that $|z|=1$, that is, such that $z \bar{z}=1$, compute $|1+z|^{2}+|1-z|^{2}$.
Proof.
$|1+z|^2=(1+z)(1+\bar{z})=1+\bar{z}+z+z \bar{z}=2+z+\bar{z}$. Similarly $|1-z|^2=(1-z)(1-\bar{z})=1-z-\bar{z}+z \bar{z}=2-z-\bar{z}$. Hence
$$
|1+z|^2+|1-z|^2=4 \text {. }
$$
Qed.
